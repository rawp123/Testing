Trend Label Definitions

Labels are self-relative ‚Äî each MDL is measured against its own average caseload, so a large MDL must change proportionally more to be flagged as rapidly moving.

TREND DIRECTION

‚¨Ü Rapidly Rising Monthly slope > +10% of the MDL's own average ‚Äî an unusually fast acceleration. ‚Üë Rising Monthly slope between +3% and +10% ‚Äî steady, notable growth. ‚Üí Stable Monthly slope within ¬±3% ‚Äî no meaningful directional change. ‚Üì Declining Monthly slope between ‚àí3% and ‚àí10% ‚Äî steady, notable decrease. ‚¨á Rapidly Declining Monthly slope < ‚àí10% of the MDL's own average ‚Äî an unusually fast contraction.

ACCELERATION (ACCEL COLUMN)

‚Üó Accel. The rate of change is speeding up ‚Äî second half of the window moved faster than the first. A rising MDL getting faster, or a declining MDL accelerating downward. ~ Steady The rate of change is consistent throughout the window ‚Äî no meaningful difference between early and recent months. ‚Üò Decel. The rate of change is slowing down ‚Äî second half of the window moved slower than the first. A declining MDL may be stabilizing; a rising one may be peaking.
