FAQ

Questions about saving iPhone messages locally.

Short, plain-English answers for Mac and iPhone users considering Message Archive Utility. For the step-by-step version, read how it works.

Does anything get uploaded?

No. Message Archive Utility is a local Mac app. It does not upload your messages for message processing.

Where are my messages stored?

The imported archive is stored locally on your Mac. You stay in control of the archive and the files you export from it.

Does it work with encrypted iPhone backups?

Not yet. The first release is being prepared around local backups the app can safely read. The site will be updated if that changes later.

Does it export attachments?

Not yet. Exports focus on message text and related details. Attached photos, videos, and files are not included in the current export promise.

Does it work on Windows?

No. The current release path is Mac-first.

Does it use iCloud?

No. The app works from a local iPhone backup on your Mac. It does not import directly from Apple's cloud services or keep syncing with your phone.

What formats can I export?

You can export PDF, Excel, and CSV files. PDF is easiest to read or print, Excel is useful for review, and CSV is a simple spreadsheet-friendly option. See the export examples.

Can I search old conversations?

Yes. After import, you can search your local archive for words, names, and phrases, then review matching messages and summary stats.

Is this a formal records tool?

No. Message Archive Utility is a consumer archive tool for reading, searching, and exporting your own messages. It is not presented as a tool for official proceedings.

What happens if I delete the app?

Deleting the app may remove the program itself, but you should not rely on that as your backup plan. Keep copies of any exports or archives you want to preserve.

Is this available in the Mac App Store?

Not currently. Release details are still being prepared.

Why does the app need to be downloaded?

The app needs to run on your Mac so message processing can happen locally instead of through a website.

Why does the app need a local iPhone backup?

The local backup gives the app a message source on your Mac. That is how it builds a searchable archive without asking you to upload messages.
