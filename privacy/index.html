Privacy

Your messages stay on your Mac.

Message Archive Utility is designed for local use. It helps you work with an archive stored on your own computer, and it does not upload messages for processing.

Summary

The app is built around a simple promise: your message archive belongs on your Mac, under your control. You decide what to import, what to search, and what to export.

What stays local

Your imported message archive is stored locally on your Mac. The app works with that local archive so you can browse conversations, search keywords, review summary stats, and create exports.

What the app imports

The app imports message information from a local iPhone backup that you choose on your Mac. It is meant to make your messages easier to read, search, and save in common file formats.

What the app does not upload

Message Archive Utility does not upload your messages for message processing. The core workflow happens on your Mac, not on a remote message review service.

Exports

You control exports. When you create a PDF, Excel workbook, or CSV file, that exported file is yours to save, move, delete, or share.

Backups and exported files

You are responsible for where you keep backups and exported files after they are created. If you copy an export to another folder, device, email, or service, that copy is outside the app's control.

Support and questions

Public support details are still being prepared. For now, release and support information will be added before the public download is available.
