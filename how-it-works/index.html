Walkthrough

Save iPhone messages to your Mac, then search and export.

Message Archive Utility gives you a guided path: prepare a local iPhone backup, import messages, search old conversations, and export files you can read and keep.

Overview

A calm workflow for preserving messages.

The app is meant for ordinary Mac users. It explains each step in plain language, keeps the work local, and makes the archive useful without asking you to learn technical terms. You can also compare export formats.

Step 1

Create or locate an iPhone backup on your Mac

Start with a local iPhone backup already on your Mac, or follow the app's guidance to make one. The goal is to give the app a local source it can read from your computer.

Step 2

Let the app guide the import

The import flow helps you choose the right local backup and explains what is happening as the archive is created. You do not need to sort through technical files yourself.

Step 3

Search your archive

Search for a word, name, or phrase and see matching messages first. When you want more context, open summary stats to see who mentioned it, where it appeared, and when it came up.

Step 4

Export PDF, Excel, or CSV

Export the full archive, one conversation, search results, summary reports, or messages with a selected person. PDF is for reading and printing, Excel is for review, and CSV is a simple spreadsheet-friendly option.

What the app does not do

  • It does not pull messages from Apple's cloud services.
  • It does not automatically keep syncing with your phone.
  • It does not upload your messages for processing.
  • It does not currently include attached photos or files in exports.
  • It does not position exports as official records for a formal proceeding.

Troubleshooting preview

If the app cannot find messages, the first things to check are whether a local backup exists, whether the expected phone was backed up on this Mac, and whether the app has been pointed to the right backup.

Privacy reminder

Your imported archive is stored locally on your Mac. You control what you export and where those exported files go after they are created.
